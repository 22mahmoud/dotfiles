Once a year or so, a discussion like "does $thing really belong at $place" pops up, and people usually have different opinions on those (especially when it comes to macOS, see part 1 and part 2, but there also have been various "does this belong to data or config" discussions as well).

In this case, whoever implemented those locations (wasn't me) thought it was a good default, and I agreed. I'm not going to spend any more time discussing which location would be right and which isn't (been there before, and usually there are good arguments for/against both). For userscripts and greasemonkey files, I suppose it'd be possible for qutebrowser to read from both paths though, so I opened an issue.

FWIW as a workaround, you can use :spawn --userscript with a full path to a script and/or symlink those directories to inside ~/.config/qutebrowser.
